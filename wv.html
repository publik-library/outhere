"Please tell me who is funding your project and how you pronounce Appalachia,” a sixth generation Black West Virginian asked me. This no-bullshit response to my request for an interview solidified why I wanted to go there. I also needed to experience the awe of the Appalachian mountains myself. The perception of this predominately white state is typically seen through the hillbilly lens and I wanted to understand who else lived here. So I sought out Black, brown to queer, non-binary, and trans, elders, youth, writers, artists, activists and everyone in between.

You’ll hear from Black coal miner’s daughter, a trans woman running for City Council to a trans woman running for city council and everyone in between.
